The present : the elephant in the room 


[UN estimates 1 Billion increase into city centers; meaning millions will migrate into every major city in the next 10 years]

Exponential population growth and unfettered/haphazard city expansion has led to the fragmentation of our city and mega cities in the last [exceptional] 150 years of human industrial and cultural evolution. City planning has been primarily centered around the predominant industry, and the personal vehicle (car) - a hugely successful technologicaly revolution, but quite wasteful under current standards.  Furthermore, tools for simulation have been around in complex science and engineering for 50 years are still not part of most city planning discussions. Now things are changing with the democratization of super/parrallel computing, robotics/sensors, and A. I.
